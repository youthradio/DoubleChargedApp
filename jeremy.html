At age 14, on his way to school one morning, Jeremy lit some cardboard boxes on fire in an alley behind a furniture store.

The fire department reported no damage to the store, but the owner claimed his entire inventory was destroyed. Jeremy's family hired a public defender to argue his case.

The court ordered that Jeremy complete 80 hours of community service and six months of probation, plus he had to maintain consistent grades, go to school, and stick to curfew. His mom also signed him up for counseling and a fire safety class.

  • $540 supervision fee*
  • * for each month of probation, parents are charged $90 in some California counties.

The furniture store owner claimed he was owed $221,412 in "restitution". That's a law designed to compensate victims for their losses, and in California, it holds regardless of a family's ability to pay.

At first the DA went for a felony charge, which would carry an additional restitution fine of $100, but then the charge was reduced to a misdemeanor.

The store owner's insurance covered the damages he claimed, minus a $2,000 deductible. Jeremy's mom's homeowner's insurance paid an additional $10,000, and the case was settled.
